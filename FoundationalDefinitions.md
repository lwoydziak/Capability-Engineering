# RFC-001: Foundational Definitions v0.2

## Status

Draft

## Purpose

Establish the foundational vocabulary and hierarchy of Capability Engineering.

The primary objective of this RFC is to validate the Enterprise Capability Value Chain and the definitions that support it.

## Executive Thesis

The defining challenge of the AI era is not deploying agents.

It is continuously converting human capability into reusable capability assets.

---

# Central Hypothesis

The central hypothesis of Capability Engineering is that enterprise value is created through the progression from knowledge to economic outcomes.

The proposed Enterprise Capability Value Chain is:

```text
Enterprise Knowledge
        ↓
Behavior
        ↓
Skill
        ↓
Agent
        ↓
Capability Asset
        ↓
Capability
        ↓
Business Outcome
        ↓
Revenue / Profit / Cost / Risk
```

The purpose of this RFC is to determine whether this hierarchy is:

- Correct
- Incomplete
- Improperly ordered
- Missing key concepts

---

# Core Definitions

## Behavior

A repeatable pattern of actions or decisions that contributes to an outcome.

---

## Skill

A reusable collection of behaviors that can be executed consistently.

Examples:

- Price optimization
- Proposal generation
- Risk assessment
- Contract review

---

## Agent

Agents are containers and orchestrators of skills.

They package skills for execution and scale.

Examples:

- Pricing Agent
- Contract Review Agent
- Bid Agent

---

## Capability

A repeatable ability to reliably produce a desired outcome within a defined domain.

Examples:

- Pricing Optimization
- Contract Acquisition
- Product Development
- Demand Forecasting

---

## Capability Asset

A capability that has been captured, packaged, governed, and deployed in a form that can repeatedly produce positive outcomes regardless of the specific implementation.

Examples:

### Capability

Pricing Optimization

### Capability Asset

An engineered and reusable implementation of Pricing Optimization that can be instantiated through:

- Humans
- Agents
- Workflows
- Human-Agent Systems
- Autonomous Systems

---

## Capability Instance

A specific implementation of a capability.

Examples:

- Human Expert
- Team
- Process
- Software
- Agent
- Human-Agent System
- Autonomous System
- Organization

The same capability may be instantiated through multiple capability instances.

---

## Capability Portfolio

A managed collection of capability assets that represents enterprise value-creation potential.

Capability portfolios may eventually become a primary mechanism for:

- Enterprise valuation
- Capability investment
- Capability governance
- Capability transfer
- Capability acquisition

---

# Capabilities vs Agents

Agents are containers of skills.

Capabilities are systems composed of:

- Agents
- Humans
- Workflows
- Governance
- Knowledge

working together to produce outcomes.

A capability may contain many agents.

An agent typically supports a capability.

The two should not be considered equivalent.

---

# Non-Goals

This RFC does not attempt to define:

- Capability Engineering
- Human-Hybrid Operating Models
- Enterprise Cognitive Orchestration
- Progressive Autonomy
- Capability Portfolio Management

These topics will be addressed in future RFCs.

The purpose of RFC-001 is solely to establish the foundational ontology upon which the discipline will be built.
