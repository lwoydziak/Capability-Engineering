# Foundational Definitions v0.1
## Executive Thesis
The defining challenge of the AI era is not deploying agents. It is continuously converting human capability into organizational capability.

## Core Definitions

### Behavior
A repeatable pattern of actions or decisions that contributes to an outcome.

### Skill
An encoded and reusable collection of behaviors that can be executed consistently.

### Agent
Agents are containers and orchestrators of skills. They package skills for execution and scale.

### Capability
A repeatable ability to reliably produce a desired outcome within a defined domain.  

### Capability Asset 
A capability that has been captured, packaged, governed, and deployed in a form that can repeatedly produce positive outcomes regardless of the specific implementation.  

### Capability Instance
A specific implementation of a capability (human expert, team, process, software, agent, human-agent system, autonomous system, organization).  

### Capability Portfolio 
A managed collection of capability assets that represents enterprise value-creation potential.

> [!NOTE]
> ### Capabilities vs Agents
>
> Agents are containers of skills. Capabilities are systems composed of agents, humans, workflows, governance, and knowledge working together to produce outcomes.
